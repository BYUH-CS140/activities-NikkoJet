About the Book:

BS:

  • "How to Hug a Porcupine" by Debbie Ellis is a self-help book using the porcupine metaphor for dealing with difficult people.
  • It teaches patience, empathy, and kindness through understanding others’ fears and motivations.
  • It highlights boundaries and how to handle emotional defensiveness.
  • People may react like porcupines when threatened; recognizing this can help avoid conflict escalation.

Ellis shares these tips for “hugging” a porcupine:

  1. Know Their Warning Signs: Watch for triggers like aggression or conflict.
  2. Keep Your Quills In: Stay calm and avoid reacting defensively. (p.13)
  3. Respect Boundaries: Don’t push—acknowledge fear.
  4. Understand Real Needs: Past trauma causes many defensive behaviors.
  5. Find the Soft Spot: Connect over what matters to them.
